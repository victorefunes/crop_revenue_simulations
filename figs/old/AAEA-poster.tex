\begin{block}{Introduction}
\begin{center}
%\includegraphics[scale=2]{milk_price.png} 
\end{center}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Research Question}: What is the causal effect of award-winning pedigrees on the price of livestock genetics?
\item \textbf{Natural experiment}: The price difference between World Dairy Expo winners and runner-ups is mostly random since the margin is relatively small.
\item Genetic traits are an important factor to determine a bull's semen price, particularly productivity and health traits (Schroeder et al., 1992; Richards and Jeffrey, 1996; Sy et , 1997).
\item None of these papers include pedigree into the pricing equation (focus on pricing each trait).
\end{itemize}

% \includegraphics[scale=2]{sample_milk_price.png}
\end{block}
\begin{block}{Data Description}
\begin{itemize}
\item 5367 quarterly evaluation observations from 476 distinct bulls belonging to five different lineages reconstructed from sire/dam data.
\item Ancestry data was merged with bull-level evaluation data from 2001 to 2004 (years of high winner turnover).
\item Source: National Association of Animal Breeders and winner/runner-up data from the World Dairy Expo (Held on October of every year).
\end{itemize}
\end{block}

\begin{block}{Empirical Model}
\begin{equation*}
price_{it} = \sum_{k=-3,k\neq -1}^{3}\beta_{k}\tau_{t+k}w_{i}+\mathbf{X}_{it}'\Gamma+\gamma_{i}+\lambda_{t}+\epsilon_{it}
\end{equation*}

\quad \\

\vspace{-1cm}
\begin{itemize}
\item $price_{it}$: Semen price for bull $i$ at time $t$.
\item $\tau_{t+k}$: Lags and leads of the treatment indicator (1 if bull $i$ is part of the winner network at time $t=0$).
\item $w_{i}$: Genetic distance to the winner/runner-up, defined as $2^{-p}$ where $p=1$ for half-brothers, $p=2$ for cousins, etc. 
\item $\mathbf{X}_{it}$ is the set of genetic traits.
\item $\gamma_{i}$, $\lambda_{t}$: bull and quarter fixed effects.
\end{itemize}


\end{block}

\begin{block}{Conclusions}
\begin{itemize}
\item Reported bull semen price responds positively to winning the World Dairy Expo, bulls on the winner network have a 10\% increase in semen prices after the event.
\item Winning the WDE impacts close relatives as well, but its short-lived and disappears by the quarter previous to the next Expo.
\end{itemize}
